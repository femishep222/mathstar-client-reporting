  • Access: Reveal the ability that traditional formats miss (ADHD, anxiety, learning differences).

Why this matters for Ellis

  • Fits MTSS: short-cycle intervention with built-in progress monitoring.
  • Supports inclusive practices: expands access to rigorous learning experiences for students with disabilities.
  • Maximizes instructional minutes: calmer sessions increase time-on-task during learning blocks.
  • Positive student demand signal: students asked for more learning time.

Week 1 reflections + refinements for Weeks 2–4

  • Add more arithmetic manipulatives for students who need additional supports.
  • Keep students seated for the first 30 mins of VR (focus + safety + personal space).
  • Set clear, student-level progress goals (MTSS-ready, easy to track).
